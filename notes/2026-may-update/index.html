Website redesign, our Manifesto, and other updates — May 2026

01 May 2026

A few things worth sharing from the past few months.

A refreshed website

We gave the site a long-overdue update — cleaner, easier to navigate. Not much more content – the goal is as always: be clear about who we are, keep the site simple, don’t make a pitch.

We published a Manifesto

The Fenris Manifesto is our attempt to say clearly what we think about data, power, and who gets to benefit from both.

We published it knowing that principles documents have become cheap. Companies, especially tech companies, have spent years issuing values statements that they later walked back. If anything, that makes it more important to us — not less — to say clearly what we believe and be willing to be held to it.

Tracking climate committments

The World Resources Institute Climate Watch team has the important and arduous task of parsing, analyzing and tracking the commitments made by nations around the world through Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). These are the climate commitments countries submit every five years under the Paris Agreement. You can explore this data at ClimateWatchData.org.

We worked with the analysis team to develop an LLM-assisted workflow that has “substantially streamlined” the NDC analysis process. According to the team, the tool reduced processing times for the target analysis by an estimated 20–25%, and simplified several previously manual data-processing steps.

AI Research tool and LLM evaluations

Recently, we helped the Product Studio at WRI release an AI-enhanced search tool for the organization’s own research outputs, called WRI Knowledge Products. We originally developed a prototype in 2025 with promising results, and this year worked with Product Studio designers and engineers to bring the idea to life. The tool is now live for limited internal use, further testing, and stakeholder demonstrations.

For these and other teams at WRI, the goal is to augment human experts and make their work easier and of greater quality, not to replace them. WRI has a high standard for research and integrity, including sensitivity to issues like hallucinations and sycophancy, which shapes the architecture and design the systems they create.

This approach is well-aligned with how Fenris is approaching this fast-moving moment in LLM technologies.

More on AI, soon

There are many reasons to dislike and mistrust AI technologies. Each of us at Fenris is both a proponent and a skeptic. Just as with data technologies more generally, these systems have to be approached responsibly.

The field is evolving rapidly and we’ve been paying close attention. We’re trying to separate the ephemeral from the durable, to develop perspectives that won’t have to change every few weeks.

LLM evaluations have become a a core part of Fenris’s work with AI technologies, and we are building expertise in developing evaluations for geospatial datasets and for mission-driven organizations with MEL practices.

(MEL stands for Measurement, Evaluation and Learning.)

We’ll write more soon about what we’re learning.

If any of this connects to what you’re working on, we’d love to hear from you.
